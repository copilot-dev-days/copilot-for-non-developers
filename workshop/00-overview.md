# 🤖 GitHub Copilot for Non-Developers: Work, Assessment, and more!

> **Duration:** ~50 minutes
> **Level:** Beginner — no coding required  
> **Audience:** BPMs, PMs, Ops & Strategy, anyone managing email and docs

---

## 🎯 What You'll Learn

By the end of this workshop you will be able to:

| # | Skill | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | **Navigate a terminal** | Understand what you're looking at when a developer shares a screen |
| 2 | **Run Copilot CLI** | Talk to your computer in plain English instead of clicking through menus |
| 3 | **Connect to Microsoft 365** | Let Copilot reach your email, calendar, Teams, and docs via MCP |
| 4 | **Triage email in seconds** | Turn a chaotic inbox into a prioritized action list |
| 5 | **Extract decisions from meetings** | Get a clean summary with owners and due dates |
| 6 | **Analyze data without Excel** | Surface insights from CSVs in plain language |

---

## ✅ Pre-Workshop Checklist

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- [ ] A computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
- [ ] [GitHub account](https://github.com) with **GitHub Copilot** enabled (Pro, Business, or Enterprise)
- [ ] A **Microsoft 365 account** (for email/calendar/Teams demos)
- [ ] A terminal open and ready (see Part 1 if you're not sure how to open one)

> 💡 **Not sure if Copilot is enabled?** Go to [github.com/settings/copilot](https://github.com/settings/copilot) and check your subscription status.

## 💡 A Note Before You Start

This workshop is **not** a coding tutorial. You will not write any code.

Everything you do in this workshop is a plain-English conversation with your computer — the same way you'd type a message in Teams or search in Google, just in a slightly different window.

> "Text your computer instead of clicking through menus."
